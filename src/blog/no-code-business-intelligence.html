No Code & BI: How AI is Shaping Business Intelligence

 

Businesses need data insights to drive growth. While relevant data might be available, the technical expertise required to transform it into meaningful insights is often a challenge. This is where no code solutions for business intelligence (BI) come in; they empower non-technical colleagues to get insights from data without the need to write code.

According to Gartner, the number of people using no code tools has been experiencing double-digit growth year-on-year since 2021, showing mass adoption by large corporations and small businesses. This trend demonstrates no code solutions' ability to improve efficiency, reduce turnaround time for generating insights, and reduce the workload of in-house analysts.

This article discusses the role of no code in business intelligence, as well as its benefits and applications across various departments and industries.

 

The No Code Movement & its Benefits

James Martin, an English author and IT consultant, predicted a shortage of tech skills in the 1980s in his book Application Development Without Programmers. He postulated computers will eventually have to be used without programmers.

Around the time of his prediction, the first version of Excel—a low code tool, was released. Excel popularized the concept of analyzing data without writing code and became a standard data analysis tool in various industries.

Recent advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) have further enhanced no code business intelligence tools, enabling users with zero technical experience to analyze data by conversing in natural language. Interestingly, more than one-third of no code users are technical professionals who use these tools to boost efficiency. 

More businesses are embracing no code business intelligence software because it offers several benefits, which include; 

Democratizing Data

AI business intelligence tools democratize data analysis, visualization, and reporting. These tools enable diverse team members, regardless of their technical skills, to access, analyze, and interpret data effectively. 

A user-friendly interface with pre-built and customizable templates makes exploring and interacting with data easy for everyone. This inclusiveness eliminates technical barriers and encourages independent data-driven decisions.

Empowers Employees

When employees have easy access to data and can independently generate insights, they develop a deeper sense of belonging within the team. This enhanced accessibility fosters greater collaboration, improves decision-making, and encourages collective problem-solving.

As employees interact more with data, they become inclined to develop analytical skills by seeking additional knowledge. Soon, they take on more complex tasks and contribute meaningfully to their teams and organizations.

Drives Smarter Decisions and Greater Impact

Some of the most successful companies leverage data to their advantage in decision-making. Imagine the impact if everyone in your company became a data champion. 

Each employee's decisions influence the entire organization. Poor decisions can set the business back significantly, causing a loss of competitive advantage, while the right decisions can provide a substantial boost to the bottom line.

With AI-powered business intelligence tools, each department can become more data-driven by conducting their analyses independently, without relying on the data team. These tools provide accurate analyses, enabling technical and non-technical staff to make impactful contributions, thus improving business outcomes.

Saves Cost 

The traditional method of hiring more talent to tackle the growing list of tasks in the data analyst backlog leads to an inflated employee roster. Also, the high demand for skilled professionals who can write code makes this approach expensive.

Integrating AI business intelligence tools into your business eliminates the need for a large team of data analysts or third-party consultants, significantly reducing associated costs. Additionally, it minimizes expenses related to fixing bugs and regular maintenance.

Easy to Integrate and Navigate

Most no code business intelligence platforms can connect to various databases for real-time analysis. Their built-in connectors support multiple data storage platforms that automatically sync when changes are made to the database.

Non-technical users can seamlessly navigate the platforms, generate reports, create dashboards, and perform other tasks without tutorials or external interventions.

Reduces Bottlenecks

The typical timeframe to turn business questions into data-driven decisions takes about 2-6  months. In addition, only individuals with technical skills are privy to handling data, and so the process is subject to their prerogative.

Integrating no code BI tools, such as BlazeSQL, into your business helps to reduce bottlenecks. As the business grows and more data is collected, scaling is easy because there will be no need for time-consuming refactoring or system overhauls.

Artificial intelligence in BI is enhancing the effectiveness of various industries and departments, enabling optimal performance through data-informed decision-making.

Tech

For software products, such as SaaS applications and mobile apps, AI business intelligence can provide product analytics. By integrating data from various sources, companies can track user engagement, monitor app performance, and identify trends in user behavior. These insights enable developers to make data-driven decisions to enhance product offerings.

Manufacturing

In manufacturing, AI finds its place in ensuring the smooth running of the manufacturing cycle. Data gathered from different machines and sensors is integrated into a single point to monitor the overall health of the machine. This saves costs significantly by avoiding breakdowns that could halt the manufacturing process. 

In quality control, collecting and analyzing production data in real-time can detect defects and abnormalities in a batch of products. It can also identify the root cause of the defect and generate a detailed report to avoid such in the future.

The Future of No Code & Business Intelligence

More companies are adopting the no code solution to become agile and in tune with the evolving business climate. In 2023, the global no code market was estimated to be about $3.83 billion, with the US contributing the largest quota of $1.2 billion. Grand View Research forecasts a continued constant annual growth rate of 30.3% for the next six years. 

According to IDC, businesses leveraging advanced intelligence observed significantly more positive outcomes than others. From 2020 to 2022, these companies saw 2.7 times more revenue growth and expedited the launch of new products, services, and initiatives 3.6 times more.  This is in tandem with the point raised by Jill Chase, a partner at CapitalG, “Generative AI is going to usher in massive productivity gains and improved cost structures for businesses of all types….”

Also, a publication by Harvard Business Review highlights how AI is helping businesses efficiently redesign their operational processes, leading to better decisions and outcomes. The publication projected that AI Business Intelligence tools will become the gold standard for business analytics. Since AI is a means to an end, smart businesses that take advantage of it will get the best of it.

As AI business intelligence becomes more mainstream, Forbes predicts the emergence of new job roles designed to enhance collaboration between humans and AI. These roles are likely to include AI trainers, AI operations specialists, AI ethicists, and data privacy experts.
